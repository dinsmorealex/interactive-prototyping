Lower East Side Printshop is pleased to introduce three new editions by renowned
 artist Ryan McGinness. Published by the Printshop through its invitational Publishing
Residency Program, Untitled (Fluorescent Women Parts) 1-3 are currently featured
 in his solo show Figure Drawings at Pace Prints [http://r20.rs6.net/tn.jsp?f=001l2y1BsQikv9JMAxEY6wIiz1ylkOo9lniHqH4bAiTjGWj_r4T1Gtv21PMUsrL7TcXG0JTEvtXiN8EkuoSaNjDJ9ShEB1TOCqUalvFs1mUqWHnMX0jlgZKDLTJHCVXgcTolSGZH7KjbkzeHi3dBqza4LoCyJXThwU7veMbxrDfP3jD9-InIdBYSazQ2nvWxQc-fsJj1nMSfuSCPke6LwDaWUufJxpt0NZL&c=6tBBRWACVeNyQy8p8kivK_0W8jMumMGt5PcEL5GH7fm_N24Ouxej4w==&ch=SOIStzNuCm3lsmFEQyC_l7aucH8jSDm6N_DMxDPDXXXPq1wsUfvL2A==]
Chelsea, and are available for viewing at the Printshop.
McGinness explores the female nude, starting with a drawing, then refining it until
he achieves a highly abstracted representation of the figure. In this playful series
of screenprints, he deconstructs the figure and rearranges its parts into a visually
tantalizing composition of shapes and fluorescent colors. The resulting prints are
equally engaging under ambient and blacklight. Please contact us to schedule a viewing.
Untitled (Fluorescent Women Parts) 1-3, 2014
Screenprint
20" x 13.5" each image, 27" x 20" each sheet
Edition of 10
Price: $5,500 each print
Ryan McGinness was born in 1972 and grew up in the surf and skate culture of Virginia
Beach, VA. The artist completed his BFA at Pittsburgh's Carnegie Mellon University,
and worked as a curatorial assistant at the Andy Warhol Museum. Known for his original
extensive vocabulary of graphic drawings which use the visual language of public
 signage, corporate logos, and contemporary iconography, McGinness creates paintings,
sculptures, and environments. He has achieved international success, having had 
numerous solo and group exhibitions throughout the United States, Europe, and Asia.
His work is included in many public and private collections, including the Museum
of Modern Art, New York, NY; Virginia Museum of Fine Art, Richmond, VA; Museum of
Contemporary Art San Diego, CA; and The Charles Saatchi Collection among many others.
The artist currently lives and works in New York, NY.
