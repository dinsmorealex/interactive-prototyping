A letter from your new
Alumni Council President

Dear Fellow Alums,
What are you waiting for?!  This is the year to participate and reconnect, whether
through attending an event, making a gift, or reaching out to fellow classmates 
in person or online. Speaking of events, this year's Homecoming is a special one,
and ALL classes are encouraged to attend the 125th Gala at Mayfair Farms. This won't
be your usual country club affair with boring speeches, you have my word.
If you've been out for a few years like I have, you gain perspective on what a great
place MKA was, and how it continues to improve, preserving grand traditions while
adding new ones. The school continues to take a leadership role in Pre-K to 12 education
under the guidance of Headmaster Tom Nammack and the entire staff. MKA is at the
 forefront of integrating technology into education. First rate academics continue,
and sports and extracurriculars are impressive (near and dear, our soccer teams 
are among the best teams in all of Essex County).
As a new member of the Board of Trustees, I am amazed by the thought and effort 
that go into making the MKA experience great for its students. There's no better
 way to thank those people who worked hard behind the scenes while you were at school
than to reconnect with MKA.  See you on October 27th. Click here  [http://www.mka.org/page.cfm?p=774]for
the alumni calendar of upcoming events.
Best,
Rich Stanton '87

A Community Grieves

The community of Montclair Kimberley Academy has come together to grieve and mourn
the loss of two very special members.
Al Rehus, a beloved and treasured member of the MKA family, died on September 26th
after a long and courageous battle with cancer.  Al was a deeply respected colleague
who served as a key member of the community and its security team for the past fifteen
years. He was a friend, mentor and role model to countless students at the Upper
 School. In 2011, the Alumni Council named him an Honorary Alumnus of the Class 
of 2011, and the following year the Class of 2012 dedicated their Yearbook to him.

Indigo Brooks, MKA Class of 2012, and a freshman at St. John's University in Queens,
NY, died on October 6th following a short illness. Indigo was one of the most vibrant
and talented members of her high school class. She was a passionate and gifted dancer
and actress, President of the Shades of Color Club, a key member of the Cheerleading
squad, a member of the Field Hockey Team, and one of the most active community service
volunteers in her year. Indigo believed in living every day to the fullest and her
energy and determination touched everyone who had the good fortune to know her.

Please join us in extending our heartfelt condolences to Al's wife Rosemary, daughter
Jamie and grandson Jeremy, and Indigo's mother, Ms. Sharon Bowles.

Young Alumni

Happy Hour

Recap

September 12, 2012

(Photo: Class of 2004)

Blue Skies and perfect weather set the stage for our annual Young Alumni Happy Hour
held on September 12th at the 79th Street Boat Basin.  More than 125 alumni, from
years 1998-2008,  joined us.  Some alumni were seeing each other for the first time
in ten years, and visited not only with each other, but with special guests including
MKA Headmaster Tom Nammack, Head of Upper School Dave Flocco, MKA's longest-serving
faculty member George Hrab,  and President of the Alumni Association Rich Stanton
'87.  The classes of 2004 and 2007 had the greatest representation.

As guests reconnected with MKA friends and faculty, they were asked to participate
in helping to raise $1,000 toward the cost of sending four juniors on the annual
 Washington, DC class trip. The generosity of our guests surpassed that goal, and
over $1,250 was raised!

At the end of the evening, the results of a raffle were announced by Headmaster 
Nammack, and two lucky alums received a bag of MKA swag and a $200 Apple Gift Card.

Upon their departure, many alumni expressed their thanks to the School, not only
 for all of the great times they had while they attended, but for the continued 
support they received since they graduated.

Check out the photos on our Facebook page and in the photo gallery on our website:
http://www.mka.org [http://r20.rs6.net/tn.jsp?e=001fmtdAklOIMramuD0p5ykjL-i2gpsqS8TL3Qsw-fTO8Eeae5TjGxDjveEAza6xoSRXJsua8FLMfVNJ1QR8aVBibSmHNUFV38Y-Qx2Cn9u25o=].
