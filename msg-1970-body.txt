We'll begin the workshop by talking about some essential lighting skills and techniques - little details that can make a big difference in the quality of your lighting. We'll follow that with an overview of the lightweight equipment we'll be using over the course and some tips for getting the most out of them. We'll also cover some D.I.Y. lighting tools you can make yourself or things you can do to modify and adapt your existing gear for more efficient use. We'll cover the essential parts of fine tuning and adjusting the lighting tools, including wireless flash triggers, so you'll have a complete understanding of how everything works before we head out in the field.<br />
<br />
The majority of the workshop will then be spent out on location, in a variety of challenging and typical settings, creating portraits that are fresh, stylish, and with lighting that is easy to understand and re-create. I'll discuss every setting I use on my camera and lighting gear, and why I choose one option over another.<br />

Our lighting process will be focused on lightweight, portable tools that most photographers can easily carry and setup with one assistant. Most of the scenes could also be accomplished with a solo photographer, given a little extra setup time. Our primary tools will be speedlights and smaller, battery powered lighting systems with a variety of light modifiers and accessories.<br />
<br />
We'll also include a more involved lighting scene so you can see how to approach similar projects and have the confidence to take them on yourself.<br />
<br />
