Creative Minute: Malaria research
Chemistry professor Kim Frederick is perfecting "lab on a chip" technology for portable, affordable biomedical testing. This summer's student-faculty research in her lab included chemistry major (and former malaria patient)  Meti Debula '16  and computer science major  Julie Bryant '16 . Here's a Creative Minute video [ https://vimeo.com/102544737 ]  on their work.

Notable
 
Anna Fox 
    
Playwright  Anna Fox '12  is seeing her Elephant in the Room [ http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/Graydon-Gund-Productions-to-Present-THE-ELEPHANT-IN-THE-ROOM-at-FringeNYC-810-24-20140722#.U9pEZBarCPR ] , about the aftermath of sexual assault, produced this month as part of the 18th annual New York International Fringe Festival [ http://www.fringenyc.org ] . She has also had works staged at the Manhattan Repertory Theatre, Williamstown Theatre Festival, and Eugene O'Neill Theatre Center (of which she's an alum in advanced playwriting).
 
Marta Cuellar 
 
Salvadoran native  Marta Cuellar '11  is bringing it home. She was supported at Skidmore by the Saratoga Springs nonprofit Nueva Esperanza del Norte (New Hope of the North), which aims to educate and bring back young leaders to El Salvador. A government and international affairs major, she has spent the last three years working for Salvadoran nonprofits. [more [ http://www.saratogian.com/social-affairs/20140721/program-helps-el-salvadorians-get-an-education/1 ] ]
 
Andrew Plotsky 
    
Andrew Plotsky '09  is featured on the New Hope 360 Web site for his Farmrun agency, which helps individual farmers and agricultural tradespeople with marketing. He says his "agrarian creative studio" designs posters, creates video, and produces photo essays to promote everything from seed swaps to local butchers to raw milk. [more [ http://newhope360.com/managing-your-business/andrew-plotsky-agrarian-creative-studio?page=2 ] ]
 
Angel Perez 
 
Angel Perez '98  wrote an op-ed [ http://m.chronicle.com/article/The-BestBrightest/147795 ]  in the  Chronicle of Higher Education  urging college administrators to reconsider traditional admissions metrics. A social work major with a master's from Columbia and a PhD from Claremont, he's now vice president and dean of admissions and financial aid at Pitzer College.
 
Joel Lubin 
    
Joel Lubin '92  was on campus last spring for a panel on careers in entertainment: as co-head of movie talent for Creative Artists Agency, he represents some of the world's most successful actors. Before becoming an agent, he ran Nick Nolte's production company, Kingsgate Films ( Affliction  and  Simpatico ). More news about him is here [ http://www.zoominfo.com/p/Joel-Lubin/530683720 ] .
 
Phyllis Miller Green 
 
Phyllis Miller Green '53  is the author of the acclaimed Fired at Fifty: A Survivor's Guide to Prosperity [ http://www.amazon.com/Fired-Fifty-Survivors-Guide-Prosperity/dp/0991475909 ] , which tells how she started her career in network TV and later built her own ad agency. According to  Bridgette Aigner Wagner '86 , "It is a must-read for young and old professionals alike and can help anyone who ever dreamed of forging their own career path or launching their own company."
