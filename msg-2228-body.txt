BK002 Bikini Kill Self-Titled 12" EP+MP3 (Bikini Kill Records)

Bikini Kill was a feminist punk band based in Olympia, WA and =20
Washington, DC. Formed in 1990, the band is credited with instigating =20=

the Riot Grrrl movement via its political lyrics, zines, and =20
confrontational live performances. Last summer, the group founded its =20=

own label, Bikini Kill Records, in order to re-issue its back catalog =20=

on vinyl and CD, starting with its self-titled EP. Originally released =20=

in the fall of 1992, the record includes four songs recorded by Ian =20
MacKaye at Inner Ear Studios, one song from the band's 1991 demo =20
cassette, and one song recorded during a live performance at =20
Washington, D.C.'s Sanctuary Theater. The vinyl version of the reissue =20=

comes newly expanded with a 34"x 21" fanzine/poster that featuring =20
interviews, excerpts from the band's zines, and photos from the band's =20=

personal archives.

LOV72 Regents Antietam After Party CD/LP+MP3 (Lovitt Records)

Antietam After Party is the full-length debut record by Regents, a =20
Philly/Virginia/D.C.-based quartet whose members have served in punk =20
and hardcore stalwarts, like Frodus, Sleepytime Trio, and Thursday. =20
The record includes 11-tracks put to tape at Baltimore's Magpie Cage =20
studio by J Robbins (Jawbox), who also took up bass duties during the =20=

sessions. Regents will be touring the West Coast with Retox in November.
