A SEASON OF GRATITUDE

Dear Fellow Alumni,

Before we get swept away with the holiday season and winter weather, I want to take
this opportunity to look back on some of the great events of the fall, many of which
are highlighted in this newsletter.

MKA Day was held on Friday, October 17th.  I want to send a sincere thank you to
 all of the staff and volunteers who contributed to this momentous event!   See 
the box below for the specifics of the day.  This is a major step forward for our
Alumni giving campaign, and I hope that we can maintain, and even grow, this level
of success into the future.  Thank you to all alumni who participated!

Following on the heels of MKA Day was Reunion and Homecoming Weekend. We had a wonderful
turnout of alumni all weekend for classes ending in '4 and '9.  It was so great 
to catch up with old friends and see so many friendly faces around town.

Finally, another successful Alumni Networking event was held on November 11th. Because
of our new structure, we were able to have small groups of alumni rotate through
 different fields of their choosing, gaining valuable, intimate time with the career
ambassadors.

SPOTLIGHT ON...MKA LEGACY STUDENTS

Welcome to our new feature, celebrating news about the next generation of MKA alumni,
current MKA students whose parents are themselves alumni.  If you would like your
legacy student "in the spotlight" please send your news to lmontorio@mka.org [mailto:lmontorio@mka.org].
 Thank you.
Congratulations to Ethan Fusco '15, son of Amy and Peter Fusco '89, who has followed
in his dad's football footsteps (Pete played for MKA 1985-1989). Ethan has broken
the MKA single -season rushing record and is the state's seventh-leading rusher 
with 1,389 yards and 14 touchdowns.

A Star is Born! Congratulations to Doug and Lauren Hyman

Kaplan '93 whose daughter, second grader Lola Kaplan, class of 2025, has been cast
as Young Fiona in the upcoming Upper School musical SHREK. Performances are scheduled
for the weekend of March 6-8. The next issue of Cougar Chronicles will provide ticket
information.

Alexander Halprin, class of 2018, and son of Virginia and James Halprin '80, received
the President's Volunteer Service Gold Award (Feb 2014).  He was also chosen to 
captain the Wayne Y travel D squash league for the 2014-2015 season.

Catherine and Larry Brown '78 are so proud of the work their twins, Hannah '16 and
Isaiah '16. are doing, including preparing for their Junior thesis.  Hannah is in
her first year of AP Studio Art with Mr. Cuneo. She has been blowing glass for 6
 years at Buck's Rock Performing Arts Camp in the summers.  She has been awarded
 the "MKA sweater" and certificate for excellence and dedication to the visual arts
including a 3 dimensional project of creating glass vessels on the theme "The Natural
Elements" under the guidance of Mr. Cuneo.

Isaiah Brown '16 was invited to UMass/Boston to compete in the 9th Chinese Bridge
Language Competition for non-native Mandarin speakers. He was among a handful of
 students selected from hundreds of applicants from across the country. In competition,
Isaiah recited his 5-minute speech from memory in Chinese before an audience including
the University Provost and a panel of judges, then fielded the judges' questions
 in the target language. Isaiah came in 3rd nationally! Mandarin is the fourth language
he is studying formally.
