A Fall to Remember

We certainly had a memorable and remarkable season on the fields and courts this
 fall, and I can't thank everyone enough for their patience, flexibility and support
in what became a season like no other for MKA Athletics.  Starting with the loss
 of two beloved members of the MKA community early this fall (Mr. Al Rehus and Indigo
Brooks '12), our students and teams leaned on each other and became stronger as 
the days, weeks and months went by.

There is much to be proud of in how the student body responded to the challenges
 that were before them, how they overcame adversity when their backs were against
the wall, and how they represented MKA day in and day out with pride and dignity.
The fall 2012 season will stay in the minds and hearts of our kids, parents and 
coaches for a very long time.

In all, 5 different MKA Varsity Athletic Teams captured an unprecedented 10 championships
 Congratulations to the following teams who won SEC League, NJ Prep and/or NJSIAA
State Championships this fall:

Field Hockey (18-4) - 2012 NJSIAA North I Group I Sectional Champions, 2012 NJISAA
Prep B State Champions

Boys' Soccer (19-2-1) - 2012 NJSIAA Non-Public B State Champions, 2012 NJSIAA North
Non-Public B Champions, 2012 SEC American Division Champions

Girls' Soccer (16-5-1) - 2012 NJISAA Prep B State Champions, 2012 SEC Liberty Division
Champions

Girls' Tennis (17-8) - 2012 NJSIAA Non-Public B State Champions, 2012 NJSIAA North
Non-Public B Champions

Volleyball (20-4) - 2012 SEC Liberty Division Champions

In addition, the Boys' Cross Country Team captured 3rd Place in the Non-Public B
 State Championship and qualified for the Meet of Champions for the 1st time in 
7 years.  And to top it off, the Football Team won their 1st round playoff game 
in a dramatic come from behind OT win against Marist.

Thank you again for all of your support throughout the fall season...I hope everyone
had a wonderful holiday season and has a great start to the New Year!

Todd Smith

Director of Athletics

Albert "Al" Rehus (1949-2012)

On Wednesday September 26, the Montclair Kimberley Academy community lost a very
 special member of our family when Albert "Al" Rehus, MKA's Upper School Director
of Security, passed away.

Al was a beloved son, husband, father, grandfather and friend to hundreds, if not
thousands. MKA lost a wonderful and genuine man who always brought a smile to peoples
faces and who always put the needs of others before his own.  He was the rock upon
which we all relied and was always there for us, whether we realized it or not.

Al loved MKA and the student athletes and coaches with whom he worked. Van Brunt
 Field and the Fairleigh S. Dickinson Jr. Gymnasium were his homes away from home,
and he made sure that they was ready for our student athletes each and every day.
His impact on others went far beyond the classroom or the fields, and although he
is gone, he will never be forgotten.

The 2012 MKA Fall Athletic season was dedicated to our friend, Al Rehus.

Todd C. Smith

MKA Director of Athletics

2012 Fall Captain's Award - Allie Bond

The MKA "Captain's Award" is given out each season to that male OR female captain
who has exhibited the qualities that we look for in our team captains...leadership,
dedication, respect, work ethic, and being a role model both on and off the field
to his or her teammates and peers.

Congratulations to Allie Bond '13 (Field Hockey)...the 2012 Fall Captain's Prize
 recipient.

Coach Han writes...

Allie is the ultimate role model.  As a captain, she never allowed herself to have
an "off day".  She helped the team stay organized throughout the season and her 
high energy level and easy going personality made everything she did seem easy. 
 She epitomizes what it means to be a team player.  She always does what is asked
of her without complaint or hesitation; whether it's against our opponent or a new
drill in practice, she attacks relentlessly and does whatever she can for the good
of the team.  She has made a lasting impression not only on the program, but on 
all of the individuals, coaches and athletes alike, who have crossed paths with 
her over the last four years.  She had an amazing ability to listen and to provide
a shoulder to lean on for everyone, often putting her own priorities and goals to
the side.

You have been one of the most consistent and strongest team captains I have ever
 had the privilege to coach and work with during my career, and I know that your
 dedication and relentless energy have helped put our team on the map and have helped
lead our team to a school record 18 win season, winning 3 of the last 4 NJ Prep 
State titles, including this year's first NJSIAA state sectional title since 2001.
