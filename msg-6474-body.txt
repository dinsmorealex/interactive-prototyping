 Notable
  
 
 
 
    
   
    
 
 
    
 Michaela Crie Stone 
    
 
 
 
Furniture by Michaela Crie Stone '11 [ http://www.michaelacstone.com ] , winner of a 2014 Society of Arts and Crafts award, will be featured at SAC's gallery in Boston from May to July. Stone was one of eight winners of a two-year art residency at the Anderson Ranch in Aspen, Colo., made the "On Our Radar" list in the February-March  American Craft  magazine, and won the top design prize at the Maine Wood Exhibition. She is currently a fellow at the Center for Furniture Craftsmanship in Rockport, Maine.
 
  Politico [ http://www.politico.com/gallery/2014/01/hillary-clintons-50-influentials/001546-022036.html ]  magazine [ http://www.politico.com/gallery/2014/01/hillary-clintons-50-influentials/001546-022036.html ]  predicts  Nick Merrill '05  will play an influential role in Hillary Clinton's 2016 presidential campaign if she chooses to run. Merrill worked on Clinton's 2008 campaign, was a State Department strategic communications specialist, and recently became her press secretary. Politico says he "has become trusted in a short period of time" and is the aide who "most often travels with her to public events."
 
 
 
 
 
   
    
 
 
 Nabila Alibhai 
    
 
 
 
 Nabila Alibhai '00  is a fellow in MIT's Special Program for Urban and Regional Studies [ http://web.mit.edu/spurs/www/program/index.htm ] . She formerly worked with the Aga Khan Development Network, the United Nations, and the International Organization for Migration. She has helped governments and multilateral organizations from Afghanistan and Pakistan to Kenya and Tanzania, helping address rural development, public health, and emergency preparedness. Alibhai holds a master's in public health from Yale University.
 
   Katie Burke '97  recently became an executive VP for global consumer-information firm Nielsen. A 20-year marketing and PR veteran, she was formerly the global chair of public affairs at Edelman. Earlier she directed television news in the White House and oversaw communications for political campaigns including Rudy Giuliani's presidential bid and Arnold Schwarzenegger's gubernatorial reelection campaign. Burke has a master's from the London School of Economics. [more [ http://www.nielsen.com/us/en/press-room/2014/nielsen-appoints-katie-burke-evp-of-global-marketing-and-communications.html ] ]
 
 
 
 
 
    
  [ http://www.amazon.com/Recovering-Disability-Early-Modern-England/dp/0814212158 ]  
    
 
 
 
 David Wood '93  gave the keynote address at the University of Michigan's Early Modern Colloquium. Co-editor of Recovering Disability in Early Modern England [ https://ohiostatepress.org/index.htm?books/book%20pages/Hobgood%20Recovering.html ] , a collection of essays by the likes of Spenser, Jonson, and Shakespeare as well as common jest books by other authors, he's an English professor and director of his department's honors program at Northern Michigan University in Marquette.
 
 
 
 
   
    
 
 
 Liz Roman Gallese 
    
 
 
 

 Liz Roman Gallese '69  will discuss  Women of '69, Unboxed  at the Saratoga Film Forum on April 26 (before its Reunion showing on May 31). A veteran journalist and author in print and online, she's the executive producer of the film and helped interview many of her classmates for it, exploring everything from their social environment in 1965 to their personal journeys toward the age of 65. [more [ http://womenof69unboxed.org ] ]
