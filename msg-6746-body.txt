SaratogaArtsFest invites Skidmore alumni to a vibrant celebration of the arts  
  
Skidmore’s hometown arts scene gets off to a roaring start with SaratogaArtsFest, June 11-15. If you’re within striking distance of Saratoga Springs, we hope you will join us.
The eighth annual ArtsFest takes place at venues throughout the city, with a varied schedule that spans music, dance, visual art, film, theatre, and literary art. With more than 50 events, the festival is designed to appeal to a wide range of audiences, from avid arts enthusiasts to families with young children.
ArtsFest has plenty of connections to Skidmore, including the participation of our alumni. The festival line-up includes the Alumni Invitational 4 art exhibition [ http://www.skidmore.edu/news/2014/0404-alumni-invitational.php ]  at the Tang Museum; a theater performance by No. 11 Productions [ http://www.no11productions.com ] , a New York City-based troupe of Skidmore alums; and a screening of the documentary Fame High [ http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JbHJXY8k6RI ]  directed by Academy Award nominee Scott Hamilton Kennedy ’87. The festival also offers a program titled “Sport as Art,” led by Skidmore Professor Jeffrey Segrave, and an exhibition of paintings by Professor Emeritus David Miller.
Other highlights are the acclaimed Martha Graham Dance Company [ http://marthagraham.org ]  at the Saratoga Performing Arts Center and Grammy and Tony winner Duncan Sheik [ http://www.duncansheik.com ]  at Skidmore’s Arthur Zankel Music Center.
 Admission to most festival events is free with the purchase of a SaratogaArtsFest ARTSPASS . For some of the major events, the ARTSPASS provides an admission discount. For more information or to purchase an ARTSPASS, go to www.SaratogaArtsFest.org [ http://www.SaratogaArtsFest.org ] .
  We hope you can join us for SaratogaArtsFest 2014!
