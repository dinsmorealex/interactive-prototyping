 Faculty bookshelf
  
 
 
 
    
  [ http://www.amazon.com/Hurt-Pain-Literature-Suffering-Body/dp/1441174486 ]  
    
 
 
 
Hurt and Pain: Literature and the Suffering Body [ http://www.bloomsbury.com/us/hurt-and-pain-9780567558459 ] , by Susannah Mintz (English), draws on disability theory and recent studies of the language of pain to examine authors' portrayals of physical suffering from the 17th century to the present. The book demonstrates that through literature pain can be a source of connection, compassion, artistry, and knowledge.

 
 
 
  [ http://www.amazon.com/Venera-Poems-Jay-Rogoff/dp/0807154296 ]  
    
 
 
 
Jay Rogoff (English) has a new book of poetry, Venera [ http://lsupress.org/books/detail/venera ] , which considers various forms of love. The subjects of these poems range from a husband consoling a wife and a daydream about a childhood crush, to Abraham's fear of God yet love for Isaac and the Virgin Mary's thoughts about purity.

 Notable
  
 
 
 
   
    
 
 
 
American studies major  Samantha Levine '09  minored in art, developing unique jewelry designs featuring sterling-silver shavings. While in law school at Western New England University, she continued designing at a nearby metals studio and launched her own company, Auburn Jewelry [ http://auburn-jewelry.com ] . Levine crafts all of the pieces by hand—earrings, cufflinks, bracelets, rings, necklaces, and more—in silver and gold. Her work has appeared in CBS New York [ http://newyork.cbslocal.com/top-lists/6-great-nyc-gifts-for-grads ] , Wag Magazine [ http://www.wagmag.com/chic-choices ] , Lucky.com [ http://contributors.luckymag.com/post/auburn-jewelry ] , and Uber Apparatus [ http://uberapparatus.com/key-heart-auburn-jewelry ] .
 
 
 
 
 
    
  [ http://www.amazon.com/Gazpacho-Nacho-Tracey-C-Kyle/dp/1477817271 ]  
    
 
 
 
 Tracey Meltzer Kyle '91  has authored a bilingual children's book. Gazpacho for Nacho [ http://www.gazpachofornacho.com ]  uses rhyme to tell the tale of a finicky eater who discovers new food choices during a trip to the vegetable market. The book includes a glossary of Spanish words and a recipe for gazpacho. An eighth-grade Spanish teacher, Kyle says memories of studying in Spain as a Skidmore student helped inspire the book [ http://www.amazon.com/Gazpacho-Nacho-Tracey-C-Kyle/dp/1477817271 ] .
 
 
 
 
 
  [ http://www.amazon.com/Pizza-Good-Interactive-Cookbook-Community-ebook/dp/B00GL9T7E4 ]  
    
 
 
 
 Will Pollack '90  has turned his Pizza for Good event into an interactive e-book [ http://www.amazon.com/Pizza-Good-Interactive-Cookbook-Community-ebook/dp/B00GL9T7E4 ]  that serves up recipes, stories, and how-to's for community activists. The book documents Pollack's 2006 founding of Atlanta's ARTvision, an artists' collaborative benefiting a nonprofit for residents coping with AIDS, mental illness, or substance abuse. It all began with a gourmet pizza party he threw for family and friends: When guests offered to offset the cost of food, he suggested they donate to a local charity instead. Now it's an annual event for artists and art lovers, to benefit area charities. [more [ http://pizzaforgood.wordpress.com ] ]
 
 
 
 
 
    
  [ http://www.amazon.com/Exit-Berlin-Woman-Family-Germany/dp/0300197527 ]  
    
 
 
 
 Charlotte Bonelli '78  used genealogical records in the New York State Museum in Albany, N.Y., to research her Exit Berlin [ http://yalepress.yale.edu/yupbooks/Book.asp?isbn=9780300197525 ] , chronicling an extended Jewish family's escape from Nazi Germany. Bonelli, director of the American Jewish Committee's Archives and Records Center, based the book on correspondence from the estate of a refugee who left Germany for America a week before Kristallnacht and then worked to help the relatives she left behind.
