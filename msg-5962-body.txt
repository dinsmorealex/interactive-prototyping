FEBRUARY 2014

Greetings fellow alums!

I hope everyone is enjoying the snow, though I expect it is turning to slush for
 many readers.  The snow and rain was putting a strain on the flat roofs of the 
Upper School so they experienced different kinds of snow days.  MKA admin and buildings
department are handling with their usual aplomb and will be back in the building
 on Monday.

I also presume you are watching a fair amount of winter Olympics. Some great winter
sports are taking place at MKA and hopefully Boy's hockey will be skating in the
 McGinnis Cup Final this Saturday.
We have a lot of great Alumni activities coming up this Spring including the New
 York City reception on April 29th and the Spring Social in Montclair on May 8th.
Please avail yourselves of these great opportunities to meet or reconnect with fellow
alums.

Lastly, please participate at some level in 2013-2014 Annual Giving.  The School
 has always relied on alumni donors such as yourself to make the MKA experience 
exceptional.  Better yet, get in touch with the Development Office to volunteer 
at the next phonathon or April 9th or offer to take home a portfolio of call sheets
and place the calls from the comfort of your own home.  Also, the Muenster Field
 pavers purchase deadline is approaching so if that has been on your to do list,
 now is the time!

Spring is nigh, so get out and connect to MKA!

Rich Stanton

President

NETWORKING SERIES RECAP -
ENTREPRENEURISM

l-r:  LeRoy Watkins '99, Bryan Becker '96, Moderator, Melissa Cohn '78, Danielle
 Singer Zachman '97 and Lauren Moses '98

The second alumni networking event of the year was held at The Millenium Hotel in
New York City (Max Rudnick '07, a sales manager there, assisted us with the planning).
 An invitation was extended to current MKA parents and parents of alumni who graduated
within the last five years to expose them to the type of services our Alumni Relations
office provides.  Our guests were treated to a thoughtful, lively, one-hour moderated
panel discussion and Q&A on the topic of entrepreneurism, followed by a one-hour
 reception where alumni socialized and networked in small, intimate groups. Nearly
24 guests braved frigid temperatures to attend, with alumni years 1975 to 2009 represented.

The Entrepreneurship panel consisted of Bryan Becker '96, CEO Becker Brothers, LLC;
Danielle Singer Zachman '97, President and head designer of Danielle Stevens Jewelry,
Sydney Sumber by Danielle Stevens and Violetta; Lauren Moses '98, Founder of The
 Moses Group, and LeRoy Watkins '99, founder of MyBike, Viking Sports Camp. The 
panel was moderated by Melissa Cohn '78, Executive Vice  President, The Manhattan
Division, Guaranteed Rate.

MKA ON THE ROAD - FLORIDA

Headmaster Tom Nammack, joined by Director of Development Geoff Branigan, and the
Alumni Relations team of Lois Montorio and Gretchen Berra visited with Florida alumni
in January.

Headmaster Tom Nammack in Winter Garden with Jean Olmstead Witherington '54, Hon.
William Grant '45 and Mary Elizabeth Grant, Doug Donald '62

First stop on the road trip was Winter Garden where we enjoyed a spectacular dinner
at Chef's Table with the Hon. William Grant '45 and his lovely wife Mary Elizabeth,
Jean Olmstead Witherington '54 and Doug Donald '62.  We enjoyed an evening filled
with great conversation, laughter and beautiful toasts to all of us recited by Doug.

Vero Beach, l-r:  Doris Krebs Barnard '54, Joan Wallace Bryant '57, Patricia and
 Dan Emerson '42, Jake Jacobus '54, Helen Bryant Perry '58, Ralph Perry, Gail Garnar
Jacobus 58, Headmaster Tom Nammac (not pictured: Elizabeth Hasbrouck Cole '56 and
Robert)

Travelling down the coast to Vero Beach, we stopped in for another delicious dinner
at The Tides of Vero Beach with Daniel Emerson '42 with his wife Patricia, Doris
 Krebs Barnard '54, Jake Jacobus '54 and his wife Gail Garnar Jacobus '58, Elizabeth
Hasbrouck Cole '56 with her husband Robert, Joan Wallace Bryant '57, Helen Bryant
Perry '58 and her husband Ralph.  We thoroughly enjoyed our time with these close
friends and family who warmly welcomed us into their circle.

Lydia Keyser Nabucco '70 proudly displays the patch she saved from her Kimberley
 School blazer.

Our final destination was Boca Raton where we were joined by A. Coningsby, Jr., 
'53 with his wife Bette, Stephen Thieberg '55 and his wife Brenda, Tam Knight '57,
Lydia Nabuco '70 and Adam Shapiro '02 with his wife Ali at Seasons 52, and we thoroughly
enjoyed our time visiting with our special Boca Raton alumni.
