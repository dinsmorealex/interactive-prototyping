THE EDITIONS / ARTISTS' BOOKS FAIR TRIUMPHANT RETURN

MARKED BY STRONG ATTENDANCE AND SALES

Successful 16th Installment of Fair Lauded By

Collectors, Curators and Enthusiasts Alike

NEW YORK, NY (November 19, 2014) - Collectors, curators, and book aficionados hailed
the 16th installment of the Editions/Artists' Books Fair (E/AB Fair) as a great 
success - a steady crowd of over 6,000 people drove robust sales over the course
 of the three-day fair. Over 40 publishers and dealers from around the globe presented
an international group of emerging and established artists' prints and multiples.

Among the curators who attended the Fair were Starr Figura (MoMA), Judy Hecker (MoMA),
David Kiehl (The Whitney Museum of American Art), Shelley Landgale (Philadelphia
 Museum of Art), Dean Woodall (Museum of Fine Arts Houston), Elizabeth Wyckoff (St.
Louis Art Museum), Nadine Orenstein (The Metropolitan Museum of Art), Nancy Sojka
(Detroit Institute of Art), Wendy Weitman, and representatives from the New York
 Public Library. Other notable attendees included collectors Lacy Doyle, Steve Shane,
Ann Fensterstock, Mickey Cartin, Janice Oresman and Goldman Sach's CEO Lloyd C. 
Blankfein, and artists Kiki Smith, David Altmejd, Alex Katz, William Villalongo,
 Walton Ford, Nicole Eisenman, Valerie Hammond, and Carrie Moyer.

"The energy this weekend was palpable," said Dusica Kirjakovic, E/AB's organizer.
"Feedback from visitors and exhibitors has been overwhelmingly positive. We are 
delighted to have been able to revitalize this beloved show and to have had noted
editor and critic Faye Hirsch curate the exhibition - reinstating it as must-go 
destination during Print Week."

Marie Tennyson of the LeRoy Neiman Center for Print Studies at Columbia University
echoed Kirjakovic's sentiments, saying, "We really missed the Fair last year, it
 is the only one we do and we are delighted the Lower East Side Printshop has reestablished
it." She added, "There is such a strong community among the exhibitors and the response
from Fair goers has been amazing. David Altmejd, Cecily Brown, Rirkrit Tiravanija
and Tomas Vu all drew strong critical attention and sales and we have museum reserves
for Rirkrit's and David's work."

The Fair was praised by exhibitors overall for its strong attendance - in both quality
and quantity of visitors. In fact, with all of the large collectors and museums 
having visited, notable sales included Composer Howard Shore's purchase of work 
by Will Sturgis and Collector Mickey Cartin and film director Jonathan Demme's purchases
of works by noted printmaker Tony Fitzpatrick at Black Shamrock Etchings. Aaron 
Spangler's work drew a lot of attention and David Rathman's Dangles & Snipes Series
prints were a big hit with collectors." Felix Harlan of Harlan & Weaver also noted
good sales - especially with Kiki Smith's latest prints, which sold well both individually
and as a set.

About Editions Artists' Book Fair

Edition/Artists' Books Fair (E/AB) (www.eabfair.org) is New York's premier showcase
for contemporary publishers and dealers, presenting the latest and greatest in prints,
multiples, and artists' books. The Fair is well known for its vibrant energy thanks
to its innovative international exhibitors, and hundreds of artists shown.

Founded in 1998 by Susan Inglett of I.C. Editions, in partnership with Brooke Alexander
Editions and Printed Matter, the fair is now presented by the Lower East Side Printshop,
a non-profit organization. It was the first fair to offer free admission, initialed
with the intent to introduce a broad public to contemporary prints, multiples and
artists' books.
