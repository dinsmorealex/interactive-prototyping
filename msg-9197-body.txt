Fugazi First Demo

In early January 1988 and after only ten shows, Fugazi (https://t.e2ma.net/=
click/dbyyk/978dc8/5ko3ad) decided to go into Inner Ear Studio to see what =
their music sounded like on tape. Despite the fact that Ian, Joe, and Brend=
an had been playing together for nearly a year, it was still early days for=
 the band. Guy had only been a full member of Fugazi for a few months and o=
nly sang lead on one song ("Break In"). It would be nearly another year bef=
ore he would start playing guitar with the band.

The sessions only lasted a couple of days, but tour dates and indecision ab=
out the tape would delay the final mix for another two months. Though the b=
and was at first pleased with the results, it soon became clear that this t=
ape would remain a demo as new songs were being written and the older songs=
 were evolving and changing shape while the band was out on tour. It was de=
cided that the session would be passed out free as cassette copies, with th=
e band actively encouraging people to share the recording.

The only song from the demo session that was formally released was "In Defe=
nse of Humans", which appeared on the State of the Union compilation in 198=
9. Now, some 26 years later, Dischord is releasing the entire first demo in=
cluding the one song ("Turn Off Your Guns") that wasn't included on the ori=
ginal cassette.
