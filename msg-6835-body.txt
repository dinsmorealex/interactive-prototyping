Lower East Side Printshop is thrilled to announce our second publication in=
 the=20

 the=20
invitational Publishing Residency Program, To Joy: Circle, To Joy: Gentle W=

The suite To Joy is inspired by Friedrich Schiller's Ode to Joy and feature=
s images
from Bremer's 1972 never-developed family photographs. These new images com=
plete
 the suite of four earlier editions published in 2012. Capturing nostalgic =
moments
from a family vacation in the Swiss Alps, the artist applies richly colorfu=
l surfaces,
hand painting, and cut-out collage elements to recreate the blissful scenes=
 from
 his childhood.
To Joy: Circle, To Joy: Gentle Wing To Joy: Virtue's Steep Hill, 2014
Archival inkjet, hand painting and collage
24" x 24" image and sheet, each
Edition of 12
Price: $2,500 each print
Sebastiaan Bremer (b. 1970 Amsterdam, The Netherlands; lives and works in B=
rooklyn,

m 1989
until 1991. He received the Werkbeurs Grant from FBKVB in Holland and moved=
 to New

rk. Solo
exhibitions include Edwynn Houk Gallery, Z=FCrich, Switzerland; Hales Galle=
ry, London,
UK; Galerie Barbara Thumm, Berlin, Germany; James Fuentes, New York, NY; Br=
avinLee
Programs, New York, NY; Hales Gallery, London, UK; and Mia Sundberg Galleri=
, Stockholm,
Sweden. His work is part of many collections including The Victoria and Alb=
ert Museum,
London, UK; Museum of Modern Art, New York, NY; the Berger Collection, Zuri=
ch, Switzerland;
and the Rabobank collection, The Netherlands. His work is the subject of tw=
o monographs
and he is represented by Edwynn Houk Gallery in New York, Hales Gallery in =
London,
and Galerie Barbara Thumm in Berlin.

<div>Lower East Side Printshop is thrilled to announce our second publicati=
on in the series <em>To Joy</em> with accomplished artist Sebastiaan Bremer=
. Published through the invitational Publishing Residency Program,&nbsp;<sp=
an><em><em><em>To Joy: Circle, </em></em>To Joy: Gentle Wing, </em>and <em>=
<em><em>To Joy: Virtue's Steep Hill </em></em></em>are available for viewin=
g at the Printshop.</span><br /><br /></div>
</span>
<div><span>
<div><span style=3D"font-size: 12pt; font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-seri=
f;">The suite <i>To Joy</i> is inspired by Friedrich Schiller's <i>Ode to J=
oy</i></span><span style=3D"font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-serif; font-s=
ize: 12pt;"> and features images from Bremer's 1972 never-developed family =
photographs. <span>These new images complete the suite of four earlier edit=
ions published in 2012<span style=3D"font-family: Arial,Helvetica,sans-seri=
f; font-size: 12pt;">. </span></span>Capturing nostalgic moments from a fam=
ily vacation in th</span>e Swiss Alps, the artist applies richly colorful s=
urfaces, hand painting, and cut-out collage elements to recreate the blissf=
ul scenes from his childhood. &nbsp; &nbsp;</div>

<div><strong>Sebastiaan Bremer</strong> (b. 1970 Amsterdam, The Netherlands=
; lives and works in Brooklyn, NY) attended the open studio program at the =
Vrije Academie in The Hague from 1989 until 1991. He received the Werkbeurs=
 Grant from FBKVB in Holland and moved to New York in 1992. In 2001 he had =
a solo debut <i>Veronica </i>at Roebling Hall, New York. Solo exhibitions i=
nclude Edwynn Houk Gallery, Z=FCrich, Switzerland; Hales Gallery, London, U=
K; Galerie Barbara Thumm, Berlin, Germany; James Fuentes, New York, NY; Bra=
vinLee Programs, New York, NY; Hales Gallery, London, UK; and Mia Sundberg =
Galleri, Stockholm, Sweden. His work is part of many collections including =
The Victoria and Albert Museum, London, UK; Museum of Modern Art, New York,=
 NY; the Berger Collection, Zurich, Switzerland; and the Rabobank collectio=
n, The Netherlands. His work is the subject of two monographs and he is rep=
resented by Edwynn Houk Gallery in New York, Hales Gallery in London, and <=
span style=3D"color: #4c4c4c;">Galerie</span><span style=3D"font-style: nor=
