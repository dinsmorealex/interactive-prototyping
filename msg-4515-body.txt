We are thrilled to announce publication of a significant new body of work by Hank
Willis Thomas as part of the Printshop's Publishing Residency.

In his new suite of prints, Rich Black Specimen, Thomas appropriates images from
 Blind Memory: Visual Representations of Slavery in England and America 1780-1865.
The book includes a variety of sources including runaway slave posters where Thomas
found these four figures. The original images are crude, generic depictions that
 consider how slaves were misrepresented.

In  the process of manipulating the images there was a technical glitch  that created
an aura around the figures. Thomas was compelled by the  hazy light around the figures
and the new view it offered.

The suite is available for viewing and purchase at the Printshop and will be featured
in the forthcoming Editions '14 catalogue.

Rich Black Specimen: #26, #460, #21, #66, 2013

Screenprint

26.25" x 20.25" image and sheet

Edition of 16
