An Email from Riders Alliance

Hi Alex: 

Metrocard fares are rising yet again--*for the fifth time in just seven years*. Riders are paying more than ever before--and subway improvements aren't happening quickly enough. 

Now the MTA has proposed its Five-Year Capital Program, $32 billion in subway improvements and repairs we desperately need--but unfortunately, the Capital Program isn't fully funded. Soon, Governor Cuomo and legislators need to answer a big question: how will we fund the $15 billion gap in the Capital Program?

For subway and bus riders, the stakes of this decision are high: if Governor Cuomo and legislators don't find sustainable sources of funding to fill the gap, our metrocard fares could rise as high as $3.41 per ride! 

Underfunding transit has real consequences for all New Yorkers. That's why we're supporting a fair solution called "Move NY", and we need you to call on our elected officials to stand with subway and bus riders. *Here are two things you can do to help:* 

1) *Sign the petition* at www.FillTheGapNY.org to send a letter to your elected officials asking them to fully fund the 5-year MTA Capital Program. 

2) *Attend our training on March 2nd:* we're meeting with city and state legislators to tell them we need a fully-funded capital plan--and *we need you!* Come to our training in Manhattan on March 2nd, where we'll plan how to talk to our legislators about funding the MTA--without burdening riders. Let's get ready to fight for fair fares! RSVP at ridersny.org/march-funding-training.

See you soon!
